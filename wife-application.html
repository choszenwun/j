An Inquiry of Intention

Wife Application

Jacob Fleming

I'm looking for a wife who wants to build a real family.

Not a modern arrangement where two people split bills and outsource children to institutions. A family where the man provides, protects, and leads—and the woman cultivates the home as a center of beauty, intellect, creativity, and children.

Women have always worked. The question is: for whom? For strangers who profit from your exhaustion? Or for a household you govern, children you're raising, a legacy you're building? I'm not asking you to stop working. I'm asking you to work for something that's actually yours. I will provide for my family completely. You will never need to sell your time to corporations. Your labor goes toward us—our home, our children, our legacy—and you keep the fruits.

This is serious. If you're not serious, stop here.

SECTION I

Vision

Q1 Your Ideal Day

Describe your ideal day as a married woman with young children, a devoted husband, and zero financial stress. You have freedom to pursue art, music, learning, gardening—whatever feeds your soul—while raising children saturated in love and wisdom from birth.

What does that day actually look like? What are you doing? How do you feel?

Q2 The Marriage Your Children Witness

What kind of marriage do you want your children to see and eventually model? What would make you proud for your daughter to expect from a husband—or your son to become as a man?

SECTION II

Core Values

Q3 Division of Labor

I believe the optimal structure is the man providing financially while the woman governs the home and children—not as limitation, but as liberation. Women throughout history who lived this way weren't oppressed. They ran households as intellectual and artistic institutions while raising children who shaped civilization.

What do you actually believe about this? If you disagree, explain your alternative vision.

Q4 Marriage and Loyalty

What does marriage mean to you? Is it permanent and sacred, or conditional and negotiable? How serious is infidelity in your view?

Be specific. I want to understand how you actually think about commitment.

Q5 The Feminist Question

What has feminism given you that you're grateful for? What has it cost you that you're only beginning to recognize?

I'm not asking which team you're on. I'm asking what you've actually observed in your own life and the lives of women around you.

Q6 Leadership and Trust

Do you believe a wife should trust her husband's leadership? Not blind obedience—conscious choice to trust masculine direction, the way any effective partnership requires someone to hold final decision-making authority.

What does that trust look like to you? And equally important: what would be unacceptable in a husband's leadership? Where are the lines?

Q7 My Duties to You

I believe a husband has sacred duties: to provide completely, to protect fiercely, to lead wisely, to serve sacrificially, and to never abandon.

What are the non-negotiable duties you would hold me accountable to? What does loving leadership look like in daily behavior? I want to know the standard you'd measure me against.

Q8 What Would Disqualify Me?

What behaviors, patterns, or failures in a husband would be unacceptable to you? What would make you walk away?

I want to know what standard you hold men to—and whether it's as high as the standard you hold for yourself.

SECTION III

Motherhood

Q9 Desire for Children

How many children do you want? I want 4-6, possibly more.

I'm looking for a woman who genuinely desires a large family—not as a burden, but as the highest calling. Does this align with your heart, or does it feel misaligned with the life you actually want?

Q10 Pregnancy as Sacred Work

My mother read to me and sang to me every day while I was in the womb. Philosophy, history, stories, music—constant enrichment from conception onward. I believe children's potential is shaped from the beginning, through what the mother consumes intellectually, emotionally, and physically during pregnancy.

I want our children saturated in language, beauty, and ideas from before they're born. This isn't pressure—it's reverence for what we're creating.

Does this vision energize you? Or does it sound exhausting? Be honest.

Q11 Full Presence

From conception through birth and recovery, you would be completely free from work. Zero job stress. Full presence with the baby. Space to read, create, nest, and prepare.

Does this sound like liberation to you—or like a cage? How do you genuinely feel about making pregnancy and early motherhood your entire focus in that season?

Q12 Your Honest Feeling About Children

What do you genuinely feel when you see babies and young children? Do they light you up? Do you feel pulled toward nurturing? Or do they mostly irritate you?

Don't tell me what you think I want to hear. Tell me the truth.

Q13 Career vs. Legacy

Do you have career ambitions? If raising a large family required you to pause professional work for a decade or more, would you embrace that joyfully—or would you eventually resent me for it?

What matters more to you: career achievement or family legacy? I need the honest answer.

SECTION IV

Purity and History

Sexual history matters. Not as moral judgment, but as pattern recognition. Every bond leaves residue. Pair bonding is real. Trust is built on truth. This section exists because honesty is the foundation of everything.

Q14 Sexual History

Are you a virgin? If yes, what values led you to that choice?

If no, explain your history honestly. How many partners? What happened in those relationships? Why did they end? What have you learned?

I'm asking because I want to understand what you carry.

Q15 Radical Honesty

If we move toward marriage, I need to trust your word completely. Do you value complete transparency, even about difficult things? Or does the expectation of full honesty make you defensive?

Q16 Your Relationship with Purity

Modern culture encourages women to give away their purity freely—to treat sex as casual, to accumulate experiences, to mock modesty as repression.

Has this served you well? Has it served the women you know? What do you actually believe about purity, virginity, and sexual restraint—not what you've been told to believe, but what you've observed to be true?

SECTION V

Attention and Presentation

Q17 Male Attention

What is your relationship with male attention from strangers? Be honest about what validation you seek and why.

What would it cost you emotionally to become invisible to men who aren't your husband?

Q18 Social Media

How do you present yourself online? How many followers? What kind of content? Revealing photos, thirst traps, curated performance—or something more private?

Do you see social media as a tool, a distraction, or a source of validation? Are you willing to make your accounts private or significantly reduce posting after marriage?

Q19 Modesty

How do you currently dress? Do you dress to attract attention or to command respect?

I'm not asking for frumpy. I'm asking for dignified—like a woman who knows her value isn't in displaying her body to strangers.

SECTION VI

Lifestyle and Health

Q20 How You Actually Live

Describe an honest week in your life. What do you eat? How do you spend your time? How much goes to scrolling, streaming, spectating?

If you tracked your screen time for a week, what would it reveal about what you're actually building?

Q21 Substances

Have you done drugs? How often do you drink? What was your party phase like? What are your current habits?

Full honesty.

Q22 Lifelong Vitality

Are you committed to lifelong health—clean eating, regular movement, protecting your physical and mental vitality? Do you understand that your health is the foundation of our family's strength?

Q23 Tattoos and Presentation

Do you have tattoos? How do you present yourself generally—grooming, style, care for your appearance?

SECTION VII

Freedom and Creation

Q24 What Would You Build?

Here's the vision: you have complete financial support, a beautiful home, children to raise. You're free from the grind.

What do you actually do with that freedom?

Do you have artistic, intellectual, or creative pursuits you'd pour yourself into? Music, painting, writing, gardening, hosting, deep reading, educating your children? Would you build your home into a center of culture and learning?

Or would you take this gift for granted—scrolling, watching TV, letting your mind go soft?

Be brutally honest about what you'd actually do with freedom. Most people waste it. Would you?

Q25 Domestic Arts

What domestic skills do you already feel confident in? Cooking, hospitality, organization, budgeting, childcare?

What would you want to learn? I'm looking for willingness to grow, not perfection.

SECTION VIII

Inner Life

Q26 The Sacred

What is your relationship with God, the divine, or the transcendent? Not what you claim to believe—what do you actually practice? Prayer, meditation, reading, worship?

What does your spiritual life look like week to week, honestly?

Q27 Your Worldview

What are your views on abortion? On marriage? On the sexual revolution and its effects on society? On raising children with clear values in a confused culture?

Be specific. I want to understand how you think.

SECTION IX

Roots and Relationships

Q28 Your Shadow

What trauma or emotional baggage do you carry? Everyone has something.

What's yours, and what have you done to heal? Be honest about what we'd be working with.

Q29 Your Circle

Who are your closest friends? What are they like? What's your family situation—relationship with parents, siblings?

How do these people influence you?

Q30 Nuclear Priority

Are you willing to prioritize our family's needs over extended family expectations, even when your parents disagree? Can you hold boundaries with grace?

SECTION X

Alignment

Q31 Your Online Presence

Link your social media. I want to see how you present yourself to the world.

Q32 Why This?

What compelled you to fill this out? Are you genuinely aligned with these values, or just curious?

Why do you think we'd work together?

Q33 The Final Question

Are you ready to commit fully to motherhood, family, and feminine cultivation—understanding it means sacrifice, reorientation, and building something that matters more than your individual ambitions?

This is not a game. This is your life and your children's lives. Are you ready?
